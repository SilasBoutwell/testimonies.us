Why Every Christian Should Write a Testimony

Your Story Matters - Here's Why

Have you ever considered the power of your personal testimony? Whether dramatic or seemingly ordinary, your story holds the ability to glorify God and inspire others. In Revelation 12:11, we are reminded that a testimony can be a weapon against darkness. "And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony..."
     A well-written testimony does more than recount events - it reveals God's faithfulness, encourages others, and strengthens your own walk with Christ. In this newsletter, we'll explore why writing your testimony is a spiritual discipline and how you can craft one that touches hearts.

Every Christian Should Write a Testimony

Why Writing Your Testimony Matters
Your testimony is more than just your personal story - it's a witness to God's power. Sharing it allows others to see His hand at work in your life and strengthens faith, both for you and those who hear it. As Psalm 107:2 states, "Let the redeemed of the Lord say so, whom he hath redeemed from the hand of the enemy."

Elements of a Well-Written Testimony
A strong testimony isn't just about recounting events; it's about clearly glorifying God. Here's how to craft yours effectively:
  1. Prayer First - Ask the Lord for guidance in sharing what He has done in your life.
  2. Structure It Clearly - Consider three key parts:
    • Before Christ: What was your life like before salvation?
    • The Turning Point: How did God change your heart?
    • After Christ: How has your life been transformed?
  3. Be Honest and Specific - Share struggles that led to victories openly.
  4. Use Scripture to Reinforce Your Experience - Example: 2 Corinthians 5:17, "Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new."
  5. Keep It God-Centered - Avoid making it all about yourself. Shift focus to how the Lord has worked in your life.


Take the Next Step - Put Your Testimony Into Words
Remember, writing your testimony isn't just a task; it's an act of worship. Through your words, someone else may come to know Christ or find a renewed strength through trials. As Romans 10:17 reminds us, "So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God."

Before you write your testimony, take time today to reflect on what God has done in your life, and start writing. The impact of your testimony might be greater than you have ever imagined!
